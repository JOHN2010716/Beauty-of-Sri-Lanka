Wildlife of Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka is a paradise for wildlife lovers, home to diverse ecosystems ranging from rainforests to dry plains. The island is known for its national parks, rare species, and rich biodiversity.

National Parks

  • Yala National Park – Famous for its leopards and elephants.
  • Udawalawe National Park – Known for its large elephant herds.
  • Wilpattu National Park – Home to lakes, forests, and rare wildlife.
  • Sinharaja Rainforest – A UNESCO-listed tropical rainforest with endemic species.

Iconic Animals

Sri Lanka is home to many unique species, including:

  • Sri Lankan Elephant – One of the largest elephant species in Asia.
  • Sri Lankan Leopard – A rare and majestic predator.
  • Sloth Bear – A shy, nocturnal bear species.
  • Blue Whale – Found off the coast of Mirissa, one of the best places for whale watching.

Birdwatching Paradise

With over 400 bird species, Sri Lanka is a dream destination for birdwatchers. The country has many endemic birds, including the Sri Lanka Junglefowl and the Ceylon Blue Magpie.
