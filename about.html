About Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka, known as the "Pearl of the Indian Ocean," is an island nation with a rich history, breathtaking landscapes, and a diverse culture. It is home to ancient ruins, stunning beaches, lush rainforests, and vibrant cities.

Geography & Climate

Sri Lanka has a tropical climate with distinct wet and dry seasons. The island is surrounded by the Indian Ocean and features breathtaking mountain ranges, vast tea plantations, and national parks teeming with wildlife.

Historical Significance

With a history dating back over 2,500 years, Sri Lanka has been influenced by Buddhism, ancient kingdoms, and colonial rule. The country is home to eight UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including Sigiriya, Anuradhapura, and Galle Fort.

Major Cities

  • Colombo – The commercial capital and largest city.
  • Kandy – The cultural heart of Sri Lanka, home to the Temple of the Tooth Relic.
  • Galle – A historic coastal city with Dutch colonial influence.
  • Anuradhapura – An ancient city filled with Buddhist heritage sites.
