Sri Lankan Culture

Sri Lanka has a rich and diverse culture influenced by centuries of traditions, religions, and colonial history. The country is known for its warm hospitality, colorful festivals, and unique arts.

Languages & Religion

The main languages spoken are Sinhala and Tamil, while English is widely used for communication. Buddhism is the dominant religion, followed by Hinduism, Islam, and Christianity.

Festivals & Traditions

  • Vesak – A Buddhist festival celebrating the birth, enlightenment, and passing of Buddha.
  • Sinhalese & Tamil New Year – A major cultural festival with traditional games, food, and rituals.
  • Esala Perahera – A grand parade in Kandy featuring elephants, drummers, and dancers.
  • Deepavali – The Hindu Festival of Lights.

Traditional Arts & Dance

Sri Lanka is famous for its traditional Kandyan dances, masks, and wood carvings. These artistic expressions date back centuries and are still performed at cultural events.
